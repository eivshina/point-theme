Research

AI for Medicine and Healthcare

My research focuses on developing artificial intelligence methods for medicine and healthcare. I'm particularly interested in using machine learning to improve disease diagnosis, treatment recommendations, and drug discovery.

Current Research

As a PhD student in Applied Mathematics at Harvard, I work in the Zitnik lab on developing AI methods for medicine. My current research focuses on:

Machine Learning for Drug Discovery

Developing novel machine learning approaches to accelerate the drug discovery process. This includes predicting drug-target interactions, identifying potential side effects, and optimizing drug properties.

Graph Neural Networks for Biological Networks

Applying graph neural networks to analyze complex biological networks, including protein-protein interaction networks, gene regulatory networks, and metabolic pathways.

AI for Precision Medicine

Creating AI systems that can provide personalized treatment recommendations based on a patient's genetic profile, medical history, and other relevant factors.
